# Where to Stay in Upper Pelling — A Neighborhood Guide

> **Quick Answer — Key Facts**
>
> - **Upper Pelling** is the helipad-side ridge of Pelling town at roughly 2,150–2,200 m — the highest and quietest of the three neighborhoods (Upper, Middle, Lower).
> - It is the **best area in Pelling for Kanchenjunga views**, because the north-west face of the ridge looks directly at the massif.
> - The Pelling Skywalk and Chenrezig Statue sit inside Upper Pelling — most hotels here are under a 10-minute walk from both.
> - Upper Pelling hotels typically cost **20–30% more** than Lower Pelling hotels; the view premium is the reason.
> - **Urbane Haauz** is an Upper Pelling boutique hotel on SH-510, ~400 m from the Skywalk, with 8 rooms plus dorm beds and Bengali-speaking staff.

If you are deciding **where to stay in Pelling**, the single most useful thing to understand before booking is that Pelling is not one neighborhood. It is three: **Upper Pelling (the helipad area), Middle Pelling (the main market), and Lower Pelling (down toward the monastery road)**. Most travel blogs treat the town as one dot on a map. It is not. The altitude, the noise, and — most importantly — the Kanchenjunga view change dramatically depending on which of the three you sleep in. This guide is a practical, honest look at **Upper Pelling hotels**, who they suit, and how they compare.

## Upper Pelling vs Lower Pelling vs Middle Pelling

Pelling sits on a terraced ridge at roughly 2,150 metres. The road climbs from the Pemayangtse Monastery turnoff at the bottom up to the helipad at the top.

| Neighborhood | Altitude (approx) | What's around | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lower Pelling** | 1,900–2,000 m | Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins, taxi stand | Monastery walks, monastery-first itineraries |
| **Middle Pelling** | 2,050–2,100 m | Main market, budget hotels, restaurants | Backpackers who want to eat late and walk to shops |
| **Upper Pelling** | 2,150–2,200 m | Helipad, Pelling Skywalk, Chenrezig Statue | Kanchenjunga views, quieter stays, families with kids |

The rule of thumb locals use: **the higher you sleep, the better the view and the quieter the night**. Upper Pelling is where the helipad, the Skywalk, and the Chenrezig Statue sit — essentially the town's high terrace. You wake up above the cloud line more often here than anywhere else in Pelling.

## Why Upper Pelling Wins for the View

This is the short version of a longer story we tell in our [Kanchenjunga view hotels in Pelling guide](/blog/kanchenjunga-view-hotels-pelling): **Upper Pelling sits on the view-facing side of the ridge**. The north-west face looks directly at the Kanchenjunga massif, the third-highest mountain in the world at 8,586 metres. From a properly oriented Upper Pelling balcony in October or November, the peak is framed clean against the morning sky by 6:15 AM.

Lower Pelling hotels, by contrast, often look up into pine slopes or sideways across the ridge. You can get a view there too, but you are fighting geography. In Upper Pelling, the geography works for you.

## Who Upper Pelling is Best Suited For

**Bengali and Bihari families from Kolkata, Durgapur, Patna, and Asansol.** If you are travelling with parents, grandparents, or small children, Upper Pelling gives you three things Middle Pelling cannot: quieter evenings (the market noise stays down the hill), flatter walking distances to the Skywalk and Chenrezig Statue (3–5 minutes on foot from most Upper Pelling properties), and far better chances of the famous morning view without renting a car at dawn.

**Honeymooners.** The quiet matters here too. Middle Pelling has a single main road that doubles as a horn-happy thoroughfare until about 9 PM. Upper Pelling is residential above that.

**Photographers.** Morning light on Kanchenjunga is a 20-minute window. Staying in Upper Pelling means you shoot from your balcony in slippers instead of driving to a viewpoint in the dark.

## What Upper Pelling is Not Ideal For

We will be honest. If your priority is walking out of the hotel at 10 PM to find a late dinner, Middle Pelling is easier — most restaurants cluster there. If you are staying one night and your whole plan revolves around a sunrise trip to Khecheopalri Lake or Yuksom, Lower Pelling shaves a few kilometres off your drive. And if you want to be on the monastery's doorstep the moment it opens, Lower Pelling is closer.

For any trip of two nights or more, though, the Upper Pelling view more than compensates for the five-minute walk down to the market.

## Walking Distances from Upper Pelling

These are the distances that actually matter day-to-day:

- **Pelling Skywalk and Chenrezig Statue**: 3–7 minutes on foot from most Upper Pelling hotels
- **Helipad viewpoint**: 5–10 minutes
- **Main Pelling market (Middle Pelling)**: 10–15 minutes downhill, 15–20 minutes back up
- **Pemayangtse Monastery**: 15–20 minutes by car (walkable, but uphill on the return)
- **Rabdentse Ruins**: 20 minutes by car

The uphill walk back from the market is the single thing first-timers underestimate. It is not hard, but at 2,150 metres after a day of sightseeing, it feels longer than it looks. This is a good reason to book a hotel in Upper Pelling rather than commute up to the Skywalk every morning.

## How to Pick an Upper Pelling Hotel

Four questions to ask before you book anywhere in Upper Pelling:

1. **Which direction does the balcony face?** The view you are paying for is the Kanchenjunga face. North-west-facing balconies get it. South-facing ones do not.
2. **Is the hotel actually in Upper Pelling, or just using the name?** Some listings say "Upper Pelling" but are 700 metres below the helipad. Look for proximity to the Skywalk on the map.
3. **What is the walking distance to the Skywalk?** Under 500 metres is genuinely walkable. Over a kilometre means you will probably take a cab.
4. **Is there a proper restaurant on-site?** The Middle Pelling market is a nice evening out, but you will want one dinner in-house — especially with elderly parents or small children after a full sightseeing day.

## Pelling Neighborhood FAQ

**Is Upper Pelling better than Lower Pelling?**
For Kanchenjunga views, quieter evenings, and proximity to the Skywalk — yes. For monastery-first itineraries or one-night stopovers, Lower Pelling is fine.

**Which is the best area to stay in Pelling for families?**
Upper Pelling. The walking terrain is flatter near the top ridge, the noise drops at night, and the Skywalk (popular with kids) is within a 5-minute walk.

**Is Upper Pelling safe at night?**
Yes. Pelling is a small town and Upper Pelling specifically is residential and quiet past 9 PM.
