# How to Reach Pelling from Kolkata: Complete 2026 Route Guide

## Quick Answer

From Kolkata, the fastest route to Pelling is a flight to Bagdogra (IXB) followed by a 4.5 to 5 hour shared or private cab to Pelling — total time around 8 hours door-to-door, total cost ₹6,500–10,000 per person. The cheapest route is the overnight Darjeeling Mail or Kanchankanya Express to New Jalpaiguri (NJP) followed by a shared cab — total time around 16 hours, total cost ₹1,200–2,500 per person. Both routes pass through Siliguri.

Everyone — flyers, train travellers, bus travellers — converges on Siliguri or its twin points Bagdogra Airport (IXB) and New Jalpaiguri Railway Station (NJP). From there it is one final road journey to Pelling through the winding hill roads of West Sikkim.

This guide walks you through each route in detail with realistic 2026 timings and costs.

## Route 1: Flight via Bagdogra (IXB) — The Fast Route

### Step 1: Kolkata to Bagdogra by Flight

Multiple airlines operate daily flights from Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU) to Bagdogra (IXB): IndiGo, Air India, Akasa, and SpiceJet. Flight time is roughly 1 hour 10 minutes.

- **Cost**: ₹3,500–7,500 one-way depending on how early you book
- **Best time to fly**: first flight of the morning (typically 6:30–7:30 am departure). This lets you reach Bagdogra by 8:45 am and start the road journey to Pelling before noon, arriving in time for sunset and dinner.
- **Booking tip**: prices rise sharply in the last week before peak season (April, May, October). Book at least three weeks ahead.

### Step 2: Bagdogra to Pelling by Road

Bagdogra Airport is about 140 km from Pelling. The drive takes 4.5 to 5 hours in dry weather, longer in monsoon or if there is holiday traffic.

Options:

- **Private cab**: ₹4,500–6,500 for the whole vehicle (seats 4–6). Most comfortable for families. Book through your hotel or a Siliguri-based operator the day before.
- **Shared cab / Sumo**: ₹450–650 per person. Runs from the pre-paid cab counter at the airport or from the main Siliguri stand if you first take a ₹200 cab to town. Sumos leave when full — generally until early afternoon.
- **Hotel pickup**: many Upper Pelling properties arrange pickup on request. Urbane Haauz can coordinate pickup if you let us know your flight details when you book.

The road goes via Sevoke Bridge, Coronation Bridge, Jorethang, and Geyzing. The Teesta River is your companion for the first half. The last 40 km from Jorethang to Pelling is the steepest climb.

### Total for Route 1
- **Time**: Kolkata airport to Pelling ≈ 7 to 8 hours
- **Cost (family of 4, approx.)**: flights ₹16,000–28,000 + private cab ₹5,500 = **₹21,500–33,500 total**

## Route 2: Train via NJP — The Budget Classic

For Bengali families, this is the most popular route because it is cheap, overnight, and leaves Kolkata at a civilised evening hour.

### Step 1: Kolkata to New Jalpaiguri (NJP) by Train

The two flagship trains:

- **12343 Darjeeling Mail**: departs Sealdah at approximately 22:05, arrives NJP around 08:00 next morning.
- **13149 Kanchankanya Express**: departs Sealdah around 20:30, arrives NJP around 09:30 next morning.
- **12041 Shatabdi Express**: day train, departs Howrah around 14:05, arrives NJP around 22:00. Faster but you arrive at night and cannot travel onwards the same day.

Fares (2026 estimates):

- Sleeper: ₹400–600
- 3 AC: ₹1,100–1,400
- 2 AC: ₹1,600–2,000
- 1 AC: ₹2,700–3,200

Book through IRCTC at least 30 days in advance during peak season. Tatkal opens one day before.

### Step 2: NJP to Pelling by Road

NJP is about 150 km from Pelling, slightly further than Bagdogra because NJP sits south of the airport. Drive time is 5 to 5.5 hours.

- **Shared cab**: ₹400–600 per person from the NJP pre-paid counter or the SNT bus stand in Siliguri. Morning departures only; later cabs drop you in the dark.
- **Private cab**: ₹4,500–6,500 for the vehicle.
- **SNT bus**: a government Sikkim Nationalised Transport bus runs from Siliguri to Geyzing daily — ₹250–350. From Geyzing you take a local taxi to Pelling (₹400–600). This is the absolute cheapest option but adds two to three hours.

### Total for Route 2
- **Time**: Sealdah to Pelling ≈ 16 hours (overnight train + morning cab)
- **Cost (family of 4, approx.)**: 4 × 3AC ₹4,800 + private cab ₹5,500 = **₹10,300 total**

## Route 3: Bus via Siliguri — The Backup

If train tickets are waitlisted and flights are unaffordable, Volvo buses run overnight from Kolkata's Esplanade or Karunamoyee to Siliguri.

- **Operators**: WBTC, Shyamoli, Royal Cruiser, Sharma Travels
- **Departure**: evening, usually 18:00–20:00
- **Arrival**: Siliguri around 07:00–09:00 next morning
- **Cost**: ₹900–1,800 per person depending on Volvo class

From Siliguri, follow Route 2 Step 2 onwards to Pelling. Budget an extra ₹400–650 per person for the shared cab leg.

## Which Route Should a Bengali Family From Kolkata Pick?

A practical recommendation based on who is travelling:

- **Grandparents in the group**: fly. The eight-hour door-to-door journey beats a 16-hour overnight train and a five-hour hill drive stacked back to back. The premium is worth it.
- **Couple or young family on a budget**: 3AC on the Darjeeling Mail + a shared cab next morning is the sweet spot. Under ₹3,000 per person total and you arrive in Pelling by early afternoon.
- **Group of four or more friends**: train + private cab from NJP is cheaper per head than flights. The car cost splits four ways.
- **Last minute (under a week's notice)**: flights are often the only option in peak months. Trains are booked out.

## Things to Know Before You Travel

- **Permits**: Indian citizens do not need a permit for Pelling itself. You only need an Inner Line Permit for certain restricted areas beyond Pelling. Carry a photo ID (Aadhaar, passport, or voter card).
- **Roads**: the last stretch from Jorethang to Pelling climbs steeply. If anyone in your group is prone to motion sickness, carry medication.
- **Connectivity**: Jio and Airtel work in most of Pelling. BSNL is reliable in patches. Data speeds are slower than in a metro.
- **ATMs**: available in Pelling bazaar but can run out during peak holiday weeks. Carry enough cash for cabs and small purchases.
- **Weather**: even in May, Upper Pelling nights are cool (10–14°C). Carry one warm layer per person regardless of month.

## FAQ

**Is there a direct flight from Kolkata to Pelling?**
No. The nearest airport is Bagdogra (IXB), about 140 km and 4.5 to 5 hours by road from Pelling. Multiple daily flights from Kolkata take roughly 1 hour 10 minutes.

**Which train should I take from Kolkata to go to Pelling?**
The Darjeeling Mail (12343) from Sealdah is the most popular — it departs Kolkata around 22:05 and reaches NJP around 08:00 next morning, giving you the full day for the hill drive to Pelling. The Kanchankanya Express (13149) is the alternative.

**How much does a private cab from Bagdogra to Pelling cost in 2026?**
Between ₹4,500 and ₹6,500 for the whole vehicle, depending on season, car type (Innova vs Sumo), and how you book. Airport pre-paid counter rates are at the higher end; hotel-arranged cabs are often in the middle of the range.

**Is the road to Pelling safe with small children?**
Yes, in dry season (October to May). The road is paved and used daily. In monsoon months there can be landslides and delays. Carry motion sickness medication — the final climb from Jorethang has many bends.
