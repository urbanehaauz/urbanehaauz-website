# Pelling in April, May, June 2026: The Complete 86-Day Peak Guide

## Quick Answer

April, May and early June 2026 are the clearest Kanchenjunga viewing window of the entire year in Pelling. Expect dry air, mild daytime temperatures of 15–22°C, cool nights around 8–14°C, and reliable sunrise views of the mountain from north-facing ridge hotels in Upper Pelling. This is also the busiest season — peak rates apply and rooms in Upper Pelling book out two to three weeks ahead, especially on weekends and during Bengali school holidays.

## Why These Three Months Matter So Much

Pelling sees its annual visibility peak in two narrow windows: April–May and October–November. Of those, April to early June is the longer, warmer window, which means it is also the window Bengali, Bihari, Delhi and Mumbai families collectively choose for their summer break. If you are planning a trip inside the 1 April to 30 June window, you are competing for the same rooms as thousands of other travellers.

As of today there are fewer than 86 days of peak Kanchenjunga visibility remaining in this cycle. By the first week of July the monsoon begins closing the horizon, and the next comparable window does not open until October. This guide tells you exactly what to expect, month by month, so you can plan fast.

## April in Pelling

### Weather
- Day: 15–20°C
- Night: 8–12°C
- Rain: occasional afternoon showers late in the month
- Sky: dry and clear in the morning, some cloud buildup by afternoon

### Kanchenjunga Visibility
April is one of the best viewing months of the year. The winter haze is gone, monsoon has not arrived, and the pre-dawn alpenglow on the Kanchenjunga snowline is visible from north-facing rooms on most mornings. Sunrise is around 5:10–5:20 am.

### What Is Special
- **Rhododendrons in bloom** along the ridges towards Sangachoeling, Khecheopalri and Yuksom. The blooms peak in the second and third weeks of April.
- **Pemayangtse Monastery and Rabdentse Ruins** are at their most photogenic — green foreground, snow mountain background.
- Cooler and drier than North Bengal and the plains, which makes Pelling a natural escape for anyone leaving a 35°C Kolkata afternoon.

### What to Wear
Layer up. A t-shirt or light shirt for the day, a fleece or light jacket for mornings and evenings, and one warm layer for pre-dawn viewing on the balcony. Comfortable walking shoes for the Pemayangtse–Rabdentse loop.

## May in Pelling

### Weather
- Day: 17–22°C
- Night: 10–14°C
- Rain: brief pre-monsoon showers possible, mainly late May
- Sky: the driest, clearest skies of the year in the first three weeks

### Kanchenjunga Visibility
If forced to pick one single best month of the year to see Kanchenjunga from Pelling, it is May. The air reaches its driest before the monsoon, and clear views of the full massif are reliable on most mornings. The viewing window often extends well past sunrise into mid-morning — a luxury you do not get in October or November.

### What Is Special
- **Longest daylight** of the main peak months. Sunrise around 4:50–5:05 am, sunset close to 6:30 pm.
- **Cooler escape** from plains heat. Families from Kolkata, Siliguri, Patna and Delhi all converge here in May.
- **Festivals**: Buddha Purnima usually falls in mid-May and is observed at the monasteries around Pelling.

### What to Wear
Daytime is t-shirt weather, but mornings and evenings still call for a fleece. Carry a thin waterproof shell for the occasional pre-monsoon shower that can arrive without warning in the last week.

## June in Pelling

### Weather
- Day: 18–23°C
- Night: 12–16°C
- Rain: pre-monsoon showers through early June, full monsoon arriving mid-to-late month
- Sky: clear mornings in the first two weeks, increasingly cloudy by month-end

### Kanchenjunga Visibility
Early June is a transitional period. The first fortnight can still deliver usable sunrise views. By the third week, monsoon cloud cover builds and the mountain hides for days at a stretch. Late June is more about waterfalls, green valleys, and lower rates than about the mountain itself.

### What Is Special
- **Fewer crowds** than April or May, especially on weekdays.
- **Lush green valleys** — the Teesta and Rangit valleys on the drive up are at their most vivid.
- **Shoulder-season pricing** from mid-June, a rare chance to stay in Upper Pelling without paying full peak rates.

### What to Wear
Everything from April and May, plus a proper rain jacket or compact umbrella. Quick-dry trousers and shoes with grip are worth it if you are planning any waterfall or monastery walks.

## A Suggested 3-Day Itinerary for Peak Season

This works for any of the three months. Adjust the early-morning timings based on sunrise.

### Day 1 — Arrival and Settle In
- Arrive in Upper Pelling by late afternoon
- Check in, freshen up, warm drink on the balcony
- Short walk to the ridge-edge viewpoint for sunset light on the Kanchenjunga line
- Dinner at your hotel (the reason a MAP meal plan matters on the first night)

### Day 2 — The Mountain and the Monasteries
- Pre-dawn wake-up (set your alarm 30 minutes before sunrise)
- Alpenglow and sunrise from your balcony or the ridge edge
- Breakfast
- Drive to Pemayangtse Monastery (2 km) and walk down to Rabdentse Ruins
- Late lunch back at the hotel or in the bazaar
- Sangachoeling Monastery in the afternoon — a steeper walk with panoramic views
- Back by sunset, hot drink, dinner

### Day 3 — Lakes and Waterfalls
- Leave by 8:30 am after breakfast
- Kanchenjunga Falls, Rimbi Waterfalls, and Khecheopalri Lake loop — about 5 to 6 hours including stops
- Lunch on the way (carry snacks; options are limited)
- Return to Upper Pelling by mid-afternoon
- Free time, short walk, or the Sky Walk and Chenrezig Statue if not yet visited
- Dinner, packing for departure

Add one more day if you want to include Yuksom and the starting point of the Goecha La trek.

## Festivals and Events in the April–June Window

- **Sikkimese New Year (Losar)** — usually falls in late February or early March, occasionally spilling into early April.
- **Buddha Purnima** — mid-May. Pemayangtse and Sangachoeling monasteries hold observances.
- **Saga Dawa** — late May or early June. A major Buddhist festival; monasteries are especially active.
- **Summer school holidays** — from mid-April through May for most Indian school boards, which is why family traffic peaks in this window.

## FAQ

**Is Pelling good to visit in April 2026?**
Yes — April is one of the two best months of the year for Kanchenjunga views from Pelling. Expect dry air, mild days around 15–20°C, cool mornings, and reliable sunrise visibility of the full massif. Book ahead as it is peak season.

**Is May too hot for Pelling?**
No. May is warm by Pelling standards but still very comfortable — daytime 17–22°C and nights 10–14°C. For anyone leaving a 35°C plains summer, it feels like a cold escape. May also has the clearest skies of the year.

**Can I see Kanchenjunga in June?**
Possibly. Early June still delivers clear sunrise views on most days. By the last week of June the monsoon typically arrives and the mountain is hidden for days at a stretch. Book a three or four-night stay to maximise your chances.

**How much does a room in Upper Pelling cost in peak season?**
Peak-season rates for boutique rooms in Upper Pelling typically run ₹1,800–3,900 depending on room category and view. Dormitory beds at Urbane Haauz run ₹400–900. Weekend rates sit at the top of the band.

**How far in advance should I book for April, May or June?**
At least two to three weeks for weekday stays and three to four weeks for weekends and long weekends. Last-minute bookings during school holidays often leave you with either no Upper Pelling options or only north-facing rooms that do not actually face the mountain.
