# Upper Pelling vs Lower Pelling: Which Side Wins for Kanchenjunga Views?

## Quick Answer

Upper Pelling sits roughly 150–200 metres higher than Lower Pelling and offers an unobstructed, full-frontal view of Mount Kanchenjunga (8,586 m), the world's third-highest peak. Lower Pelling is closer to the main bazaar and cheaper, but the ridgeline below blocks a meaningful portion of the Kanchenjunga massif. If the mountain is the reason you are travelling to Sikkim, Upper Pelling wins — and the 20–30% price premium is the cost of that view.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters

When travellers book a "Pelling hotel" on a generic OTA, very few listings tell you whether the property sits in Upper Pelling or Lower Pelling. The two areas are separated by less than two kilometres of winding road, but they are two different holidays.

Upper Pelling is the ridge. Lower Pelling is the slope below it. That small difference in elevation decides whether you wake up to Kanchenjunga filling your window — or whether you have to walk up the road with a cup of tea to see it properly.

This guide breaks down the difference across view, elevation, access, price, and atmosphere, so you can pick the side that matches the trip you actually want.

## Elevation and Geography at a Glance

Pelling is a small hill town in West Sikkim, roughly 2,150 metres above sea level at its highest point. The town is strung along a single ridge that runs roughly north-to-south, with Mount Kanchenjunga to the north-west. Because of this alignment:

- **Upper Pelling** sits on the higher spine of the ridge, roughly 2,100–2,150 m.
- **Middle Pelling** straddles the bazaar and helipad area, around 1,950–2,050 m.
- **Lower Pelling** drops down the southern slope towards Rimbi and Darap, around 1,850–1,950 m.

The north-facing side of the ridge is where the Kanchenjunga line of sight opens up. Hotels on the upper ridge have a clear horizon. Hotels further down the slope either lose the view to the ridge itself or see only a partial slice of the peak.

## The View Comparison

| Factor | Upper Pelling | Lower Pelling |
|---|---|---|
| Direct Kanchenjunga view | Yes, unobstructed from most north-facing rooms | Partial; often blocked by upper ridge |
| Sunrise on the peak (alpenglow) | Visible from bed in ridge-edge hotels | Usually requires walking uphill to a viewpoint |
| Foreground clutter | Minimal — open valley drops away | Rooftops, trees, and upper ridge intrude |
| Photography window | 5:00–7:30 am and 4:30–6:00 pm, clear line | Narrower; sun angle often wrong |
| Cloud behaviour | Clouds form below the ridge and clear faster | Clouds sit at eye level longer |

Upper Pelling's advantage is geometric, not marketing copy. You are simply higher than the obstacles.

## Access and Transportation

Both sides are reached from Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station via the same route — roughly four to five hours by shared or private cab through Jorethang and Geyzing.

- **To Upper Pelling**: cabs continue past the helipad and the main bazaar, climbing the ridge road for another five to ten minutes. Expect slightly narrower approach roads and a steeper final stretch.
- **To Lower Pelling**: cabs stop short of the climb. The bazaar, ATMs, pharmacies, and most eateries are within a five-minute walk.

Upper Pelling is quieter but requires a short cab ride (around ₹100–200) or a 15–20 minute downhill walk when you want to eat out in the main bazaar. Lower Pelling trades the view for walking convenience.

For Bengali and Bihari families travelling with elderly parents or small children, this is the practical trade-off: do you prefer waking up to the mountain, or being able to walk to a paratha shop in slippers? There is no wrong answer, but it deserves a conscious choice.

## Things to Do in Upper Pelling

Upper Pelling is not only about the view. Within a short drive you have:

- **Pemayangtse Monastery** — one of the oldest monasteries in Sikkim, roughly 2 km from Upper Pelling.
- **Rabdentse Ruins** — the second capital of the former kingdom of Sikkim, a 20-minute forest walk from the Pemayangtse road.
- **Sangachoeling Monastery** — a steeper trek, older than Pemayangtse, with panoramic Kanchenjunga views.
- **Sky Walk and Chenrezig Statue** — a short drive up the ridge; the glass walkway is the nearest thing Pelling has to a headline attraction.
- **Kanchenjunga Falls and Khecheopalri Lake** — day trips, both about an hour each way.

Most of these sit closer to Upper Pelling than Lower Pelling, which means less road time and more mountain time.

## Price Premium: What You Are Actually Paying For

Hotels in Upper Pelling typically cost 20–30% more than comparable rooms in Lower Pelling. On the surface this looks like a luxury tax. In practice you are paying for:

1. **The view itself.** A north-facing room on the upper ridge is a finite piece of real estate.
2. **Construction cost.** Building on the upper ridge is harder — narrower access, steeper land, higher material haulage cost.
3. **Quieter nights.** Upper Pelling is further from the bazaar noise and honking cabs of the main junction.
4. **Better air and light.** Thinner crowds, less dust, a cleaner horizon.

If you are coming to Pelling for the mountain and you plan to spend two or three mornings photographing it, the premium pays for itself on the first sunrise. If you are stopping for a single night on a wider Sikkim circuit, Lower Pelling is a reasonable budget choice.

## Who Should Pick Which Side?

**Pick Upper Pelling if you:**
- are travelling specifically to see Kanchenjunga
- want sunrise from your bed, not from a roadside viewpoint
- are on a honeymoon, anniversary, or photography-led trip
- are travelling in April, May, October, or November when the peak is clearest
- want a quieter, slower base

**Pick Lower Pelling if you:**
- need to be within walking distance of the bazaar
- are on a tight budget and the view is a bonus, not the goal
- are transiting through for a single night on a bigger Sikkim loop
- have mobility constraints that make the upper ridge roads inconvenient

## FAQ

**Is Upper Pelling safe to reach by car at night?**
Yes, the ridge road is tarred and used daily by local cabs and residents. The final climb is narrow in places, so arriving before dark is preferable in monsoon months. In April, May and June the road is dry and straightforward.

**How far is Upper Pelling from the main Pelling bazaar?**
About 1.5 to 2 km depending on the exact hotel. A cab ride takes five to ten minutes; walking down takes fifteen to twenty minutes, and walking up is a steady climb.

**Can I see Kanchenjunga from Lower Pelling at all?**
Yes, partially. There are dedicated viewpoints along the road where the view opens up. What you cannot easily do in Lower Pelling is see the mountain from inside your hotel room — which is the whole point for most first-time visitors.

**Is the 20–30% price premium for Upper Pelling worth it?**
For a view-led trip in peak season, yes. You are paying for the exact thing you came to see. For a single transit night on a longer Sikkim loop, probably not.
