## Step 5 — Photos (the highest-impact step)

Upload **at least 25 photos** at first launch. Google heavily favours profiles with rich photo coverage. Categorize them like this:

| Category | Min photos | What to show |
|---|---|---|
| **Logo** | 1 | Square 250×250 PNG with transparent or solid background |
| **Cover** | 1 | 1080×608 — best Kanchenjunga-from-the-property shot |
| **Exterior** | 3 | Front of building, signage, approach road |
| **Interior — common areas** | 4 | Lobby, reception desk, lounge, hallway |
| **Rooms** | 6 | One per category: Standard, Deluxe, Premium, Dorm + 2 detail shots (bed, bathroom) |
| **Restaurant / Bar** | 4 | Dining area, food plate, bar counter, tea service |
| **Views** | 4 | Kanchenjunga at sunrise, sunset, daytime, from a private balcony |
| **Team** | 2 | Reception staff, hospitality team |

**Photo guidelines:**
- Minimum 720×720 px, maximum 5 MB
- JPEG or PNG, no watermarks
- No people's faces unless they've consented
- Bright, well-lit, no filters that look unrealistic
- Rename files descriptively before upload: `urbane-haauz-kanchenjunga-view-from-deluxe-room.jpg` (Google reads filenames)

## Step 7 — Verification

Google will offer one or more methods. **Pick "Video verification"** if available — it's the fastest for hotels and Google has been pushing it for new listings.

Video verification means: Google will ask you to record a 1–3 minute video walking around the property, showing:
1. The signage / nameplate clearly
2. The street/landmark outside
3. Inside (reception, a room, a common area)
4. Equipment used by the business (restaurant kitchen, etc.)

Record on a phone, no editing, upload directly. Approval typically in 2–5 days.

## Pitfalls

- **Don't use a virtual office address.** Google requires a physical, customer-visitable location for hotels.
- **Don't add fake reviews.** Google detects them and will suspend the listing.
- **Don't change the business name post-verification.** Even adding "in Upper Pelling" to the name later is a red flag.
- **Don't share the GBP login** with non-founders. Use Google's "Manager" role to give access without sharing the password.
