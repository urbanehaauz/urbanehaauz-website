# 07 · Google Reviews — QR + Script + Templates

**Why:** Reviews aren't just stars. LLMs *read* the text of reviews to learn what kind of property we are. If 30 guests mention "Kanchenjunga view from the room", that phrase becomes part of how AI describes us. If 30 guests mention "spacious", "spotless washroom", "family-friendly", those become our brand. **Volume + freshness > average rating.**

**Time:** 30 min one-time setup, then ~2 min per checked-out guest
**Cost:** Free (₹100 for laminated QR card if you don't have one)
**Account needed:** Verified Google Business Profile (from step 04)

---

## Step 1 — Get the review link (after GBP is verified)

1. Open your Google Business Profile dashboard at **https://business.google.com**
2. Click **"Get more reviews"** or **"Ask for reviews"**
3. Copy the short link Google generates. It looks like:
   ```
   https://g.page/r/[some-random-string]/review
   ```
4. Save this link — it's the only link guests should ever use to leave a review.

---

## Step 2 — Generate a QR code (free, takes 2 minutes)

1. Go to **https://www.qr-code-generator.com** (or any free QR generator)
2. Paste the review link from Step 1
3. Download as **PNG, 1000×1000 px minimum**
4. Test by scanning with your phone — make sure it goes directly to the Google review form

---

## Step 3 — Print the reception card

Make a small card (A6 size, ~10×15 cm) with:

- **Top:** "Loved your stay? It takes 30 seconds."
- **Middle:** Big QR code (8×8 cm)
- **Bottom (small text):**
  - "Scan to leave us a Google review"
  - "Mention what made it special — your honest words help other travellers find us"
  - The Urbane Haauz logo + tagline

Get it laminated (most local print shops in Sikkim will do this for under ₹100). Place one on:
- Reception desk
- Each room (small holder on the bedside table)
- Restaurant tables

---

## Step 4 — The verbal ask script (use at checkout)

**Reception staff says, with a smile:**

> "I hope you had a wonderful stay with us. If you had a few moments and felt like sharing your experience on Google, it would mean a lot to us — we're a new property and honest reviews help families like yours find us. There's a QR code right here you can scan, and it takes about 30 seconds. Anything specific you mention — the view, the food, the dorm, the staff — really helps."

**Why each part matters:**
- "If you had a few moments" → polite opt-out, not pushy
- "We're a new property" → triggers the "help the underdog" instinct
- "Honest reviews" → don't ask for 5 stars, ask for honesty (Google penalizes review-gating)
- "Anything specific you mention" → cues the guest to write *content*, not just stars

---

## Step 5 — The follow-up message (24 hours after checkout)

Send this via WhatsApp (which is where most Indian guests live):

```
Hi [guest name],

This is [your name] from Urbane Haauz. Hope you and your family reached home safely after your stay with us.

It was wonderful having you. If you have a moment in the next day or two, would you be willing to share your honest experience on Google? It really helps other travellers — especially new ones from Kolkata who have never been to Pelling — figure out if we're the right fit.

Here's the link: [paste the GBP review link]

Whatever you'd like to share — even just a couple of lines about the view, the food, the room, or the journey — is genuinely helpful. We'd be grateful.

And of course, if there's anything we could have done better, please tell us directly first so we can fix it for the next family.

Thank you, and we hope to see you again next season.

[Your name]
Urbane Haauz · Skywalk Road, Upper Pelling
+91 91360 32524
```

**Key principles:**
- Send only **once**. Never spam-follow-up.
- Send within **24–48 hours** of checkout. Memory fades after that.
- Sign with a **real name**, not "Team Urbane Haauz".
- Include the **"tell us first if something was wrong"** line — this catches negative feedback privately and keeps it off Google.

## Step 6 — How to reply to reviews (this part matters for SEO)

Google's algorithm rewards owners who respond to reviews. AI engines also read replies — they show the property's "voice".

### Template for a 5-star review

```
Thank you so much for taking the time to share this, [first name]. Hearing that the Kanchenjunga view from your room made the trip is exactly why we built Urbane Haauz where we did. We're so glad [specific detail from their review — the food / the dorm / the staff / the kids loving it / the sunrise] worked out. Please come back next season — and bring friends.

— [Your name], co-founder, Urbane Haauz
```

### Template for a 4-star review

```
Thank you for the honest feedback, [first name], and for staying with us. We're really glad you enjoyed [the positive thing they mentioned]. Your point about [the negative thing] is fair — we're [what you're doing about it: "training the team", "adjusting the menu", "fixing the hot water timing"] and we'd love a chance to do better next time. Please reach out directly when you next visit Pelling.

— [Your name], co-founder, Urbane Haauz
```

### Template for a 3-star or below review

**Reply within 24 hours.** Never ignore. Never argue. Never delete (you can't anyway).

```
[First name], thank you for taking the time to write this — and I'm sorry your stay didn't meet your expectations. You deserved better.

[Specifically acknowledge the issue: "The hot water in your room failing on the second morning is not something we can excuse"]. That's on us. We've [the concrete action: "fixed the geyser", "retrained the night staff", "added a backup process"].

I'd genuinely like to make this right. Could you message me directly at +91 91360 32524 — I'd like to offer you a complimentary stay on your next visit so you can see what Urbane Haauz is supposed to feel like.

— [Your name], co-founder, Urbane Haauz
```

**Why this works:** Future guests reading reviews see how you handle complaints. A graceful reply to a bad review builds more trust than 50 generic 5-star replies.

## What NOT to do (will get the listing suspended)

- ❌ **Don't pay for reviews.** Google detects them and suspends listings.
- ❌ **Don't ask staff/family to review.** Google's IP/device fingerprinting flags this fast.
- ❌ **Don't gate reviews** ("only ask happy guests"). Against Google's terms.
- ❌ **Don't offer a discount in exchange for a review.** You can offer a discount for "feedback" via a private form, but never tie it to a public Google review.
- ❌ **Don't mass-send the WhatsApp ask** — make each one personal.
- ❌ **Don't reply with a copy-paste template** verbatim — adapt 1–2 words to each review.
