Your Unfair Advantage

You have real reviews. That's the moat.

No other boutique hotel in Pelling carries your average rating. Review count and average star rating are the single strongest signal for the Google Map Pack, for AI-engine citations (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude all weight user-generated reviews heavily), and for conversion once a traveller lands on your page.

Every item in this plan — schema, citations, content, GBP posts — exists to put your rating in front of more eyes. You don't need to out-spend. You need to out-surface.

Phase 1 Do Today

Under 30 minutes. Every one of these is a P0.

Paste the GBP description P0

The only item from the original PDF plan you haven't done. Copy the 742-char primary version from docs/urbanehaauz-seo-agents/gbp-description.md into Google Business Profile → Info → About. Five minutes.

Sync email addresses P0

Footer, Contact FAQ and Hotel schema still say urbanehaauz@gmail.com, but Resend now sends from info@urbanehaauz.com. Google will penalise the contradiction. Pick one canonical — recommend info@ — keep gmail only as reply-to.

Launch the review funnel MOAT

Pull the Google Place ID, generate a short review URL, make a QR code, print 10 cards, drop one in each room + reception + bar. Target: 15 new reviews by 21 May. This single motion moves map-pack ranking more than any on-site fix.

Fix blog OG images P0

All 6 posts in content/blog/*.md reference a 1 MB PNG as their social card. A 153 KB JPG sits right next to it. Every Perplexity / Google AIO citation preview is pulling the huge file. Swap .png → .jpg.

Add aggregateRating to schema MOAT

Your Hotel schema has no aggregateRating block. That means your star rating doesn't show in Google rich results. Put the review count + average there and Google can render stars in the SERP. This is the #1 underused schema field on the site.
